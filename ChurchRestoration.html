Whilst Hampton Gay is a tiny parish (there are only six households) the church itself serves a large and diverse community of people. As part of the Akeman Benefice we are a popular place for worship during the summer months, and our candlelit Christmas service sees families from all the surrounding villages walk across the fields for the service of nine lessons and carols. 
The church is also at the intersection of a number of very popular walks, and travellers from far and wide stop and picnic or explore the churchyard before continuing their journey. 
The local train line runs very close by and our little church acts as a picture perfect-flash of history as people travel across the countryside.
